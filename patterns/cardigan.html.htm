Crochet Cardigan

YOU'LL NEED:
weight 5 bulky yarn (I used 6 balls of yarn for a size small, 109 yards a ball), 8mm hook

  • FRONT SIDE PANEL:
    (make 2),working in the smallest size, larger sizes in the ( ) in order of medium, large, x-large, Chain 22 (26, 30, 34)
  • Row 1:
    (space row) place sc in 1st chain from hook, ch 4, skip 3 chains place 1 sc in 4th chain repeat until end of row
  • Row 2:
    (triangle row) ch 3, place 2 dc in ch4 space, *ch 1, place 3 dc in next ch4 space*, ch 1, repeat ** (3dc in each ch4 space) until end of row note: only the 1st ch4 space of a triangle row, gets 2 dc because the ch 3 at the beginning of the row counts as 1 dc repeat Row 1 & 2 *16 (16, 20, 24) times, chain 40 off end of 16th repeated pattern row to create sleeve place sc in 1st chain from hook, ch 4, skip 3 chains place sc in 4th chain from hook. cont. until end of row continuing evenly into the existing working pattern below.
  • Next row:
    It is a (triangle row). Repeat original *Row 1&2* pattern 3 (4, 5, 6) more times. Fasten off.
  • BACK PANEL:
    Chain 55 Row 1:
    (space row) place sc in 1st chain from hook, *ch 4, skip 3 chains place 1 sc in 4th chain* repeat ** until end of row Row 2:
    (triangle row) ch 3, place 2 dc in ch4 space, *ch 1, place 3 dc in next ch4 space*, ch 1, repeat ** (3dc in each ch4 space) until end of row repeat Row 1 & 2 *16 (16, 20, 24) times. Chain 40 off end of 16th repeated pattern row, place sc in 1st chain from hook, ch 4, skip 3 chains place sc in 4th chain from hook. cont. until end of row continuing evenly into the existing working pattern below. when you reach the end of the row, ch 40 off the other side of the back panel to make the other arm sleeve.
  • Next row is a (triangle row) chain 3, *skip 3 chains, place 3 dc in the 4th chain from hook, ch 1* repeat ** until end of row continuing evenly into the existing working pattern Repeat original *Row 1&2* pattern 3 (4, 5, 6) more times now incorporating both arms sleeves into your row. Fasten off. Connect all panels together with original yarn and hook using single crochet stitches.
