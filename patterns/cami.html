Cami Pattern

You will need: Yarn weight : 3, Hook size: 3.75mm for bottom strap and 4mm for cami body

Procedure

Starting at the bottom of the cami:

  • Row 1
    sl knot, chain 7, place 1 SC in the first chain, 1 SC in every chain of the row, Ch 1 turn
  • place 1 SC in every chain of row (6 SC total) Ch 1, turn Repeat until this strap measures your waist measurement while STRETCHED. My size small-medium measures 22 inches unstretched, and 29 inches stretched. (29 inches is the size of my waist) Join both ends together with a row of SC, Ch 1, flip, and begin crocheting along the side of strap.

CAMI BODY

  • Row 1
    Place 1 SC in the first 3 chains, on the 4th chain increase (2 SC in the same stitch) place 1 SC in each of the next 12 chains, on the chain after, increase* Repeat until end of row, connect row together with SLST, ch 1
  • Row 2,
    Place 1 SC in every chain of row, connect row together with a SL ST, Ch 1 Repeat row 2 for 3-4 rows (or longer if you want a longer cami)
  • Row 5
    (This row is the base of the bust, beginning the increases) (I'm positioning the joining chains, where all the rows connect, to be placed under my arm) Place 1 SC in every chain crocheting to exactly half of the work (this is the back of the cami) (only increase on the front of the cami for more bust space) Roughly measure to the bottom center of where each boob will be along the cami front.

As you can see I'm pulling up 2 chains along the front where my increases will be. My bust size is a full B-small C, and I'm only adding 1 increase per boob. If you want to create more space for a larger bust size, I recommend adding 4 increases total on this row. 2 increases for each boob, 2 chains apart (For an even larger bust, Row 6, place 1 increase in between the 2 increases on row 5, for each boob) *(For a bust smaller than a size B cup, no increase is necessary for Row 5)

  • ROW 6- REMAINDER
    Place 1 SC in each chain, connecting the end of each row together with a SL ST, Ch 1. Repeat until cami completely covers your bust. Fasten off.

SHOULDER STRAPS

  • Row 1
    sl knot, chain 7, place 1 SC in the first chain, 1 SC in every chain of the row, Ch 1 turn
  • Row 2
    place 1 SC in every chain of row (6 SC total) Ch 1, turn Repeat for a total of 70 rows. Fasten off. Make 2 straps total. I connect the strap sideways (acting as an edging) starting from the back of the cami (beginning where I want to attach the strap to the back) connecting with SC stitches under the armpit, to the front of the cami, where the remainder of the strap hangs off, reaching over your shoulder, then attaching end of the strap next to, and on the side of the beginning of the strap. confusing to explain, sorry.

For the remaining border which is connected to the front and back of the cami:

  • Row 1
    sl knot, chain 7, place 1 SC in the first chain, 1 SC in every chain of the row, Ch 1 turn
  • Row 2
    place 1 SC in every chain of row (6 SC total) Ch 1, turn Repeat for a total of 27 rows. make 2. Attach sideways to the front and back of the cami with SC stitches, you'll have to stretch this as you attach.
