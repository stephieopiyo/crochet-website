Oversized Crochet Cardigan

This is almost a one fits all kind of pattern. However, if you are a larger size and want it to be even bigger, you would simply add a few more rows to the initial back chain. I used 10 balls of yarn hoops & threads charisma bulky yarn in grey (109 yards per skein) 8mm hook

TO BEGIN:

  • ROW 1
    Ch 60. (my chain measures 65-70cm, this is where you increase for a larger size) place 1 SC in the second chain from the hook, Ch 1, Skip 1 chain place 1 SC in the second chain from hook. Repeat for entire row.
  • ROW 2
    Chain 2, Flip over work.Place 1 SC in the first chain 1 space, Ch 1, place 1 SC in the next chain 1 space. Repeat until end of row Repeat row 2 for entire back piece.(My back piece measures 72cm)
  • TO BEGIN ON FRONT PANELS:
    Don't fasten off once you reach the length for the back piece.Place 2 chain markers in the center 2 chains of the back piece. Continue working yarn and pattern on one side until you reach the first chain marker, stopping on a SC.
  • NEXT ROW:
    Ch 2, flip over work.(This is a decrease row) Place hook through first ch1 space, yarn over pull through, place hook directly through next ch 1 space, yarn over pull through, with 4 loops on your hook, yarn over pull through 2, yarn over pull through 2. (This completes the first decrease) Ch 1, place 1 SC in the next ch 1 space. continue pattern as normal to the end of the row.
  • NEXT ROW:
    (This is not a decrease row. you are decreasing every other row, 4 decrease rows total) ch 2, flip over work. continue moss stitch as usual until end of row. Repeat until you have 4 decrease rows total.Once you complete 4 decrease rows, continue moss stitch rows as usual until you reach the same length as the back panel. Fasten off. Re-attach working yarn on other side and repeat once more for second panel. Then Fasten off again. Place a row of SC connecting sides together to create arm space, I leave a 7 inch space from the shoulder to the armpit.
  • TO BEGIN ON SLEEVE:
    Ribbing- Ch 12, place 1 SC in second chain from hook. cont placing 1 SC in every stitch of row.
  • ROW 2
    Ch 1, place 1 SC in the back stitch of every stitch in the row. Repeat row 2, until it fits comfortably around your wrist, I completed 20 rows. (for a larger size, you could add a couple more rows to the ribbing to achieve a larger sleeve) Don't fasten off, now working along the side of the ribbing Fit 37 SC along the side. Continue original moss stitch for the remaining length of the sleeve. I complete about 35 rows for my size. Fasten off. Make 2 sleeves.
  • TO ATTACH:
    Flip body inside out, attach shoulders to sleeves, then continue down sleeve lengths with SC I also add an edging along the collar and bottom, you can refer to my youtube video! it's just a basic SC stitch.
